The Mujawar Family
About the Mujawar Family
Disclaimer: This history is hearsay from previous generations and there is no concrete proof that this was what exactly unfolded. Also, the daughters’ children are not included because they do not classify under their mother ’s lineage in Islam.

In 1799 Hafiz (last name unknown) was one of the commanders in Tipu Sultan’s army who fought against the British East India Company in the Fourth Anglo–Mysore War. When Tipu Sultan was defeated and killed, surviving commanders fled and went into hiding to avoid capture. Among them was Hafiz.

Hafiz escaped from Mysore to Nargund, later moved to Gadag, and took refuge at a local dargah where, after assuming a low profile, he eventually became known as the Mujawar of the Dargah. He later married the daughter of an Arab Jamadar visiting India, and from there the Mujawar family expanded through generations.
